Lilo and Stitch (2002)

In this heartwarming animated Disney film, a lonely Hawaiian girl named Lilo adopts a small, strange dog she names Stitch, who is actually a dangerous genetic experiment from outer space. As Stitch learns about love, family ("ohana"), and friendship, he begins to change from a chaotic creature to someone capable of caring and compassion. The film explores themes of belonging and unconditional love.

Stitch! The Movie (2003)

Often considered a pilot for the "Lilo & Stitch: The Series", this direct-to-video sequel continues the adventures of Lilo and Stitch as they discover that Stitch isn’t the only experiment created by the evil Dr. Jumba. In fact, there are 625 other experiments, each with its own powers. Together, Lilo and Stitch set out to find and rehabilitate these experiments before they fall into the wrong hands.

Lilo & Stitch 2: Stitch Has a Glitch (2005)

This is a true sequel to the original 2002 film. Stitch begins malfunctioning due to a glitch in his molecular structure, threatening to return him to his destructive programming. Lilo is preparing for a hula competition in honor of her late mother, but she becomes worried about Stitch’s behavior. The film focuses on friendship, loyalty, and what it means to truly belong.
